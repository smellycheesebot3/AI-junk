Timeline

Static site evolution

Day 1 — Empty repository
You create a repo with nothing but a README.
Maybe the README just says: "I'll build something here eventually." That alone is a real starting point — a tiny promise to yourself.
Day 2 — First index.html
You paste in a simple page like this one.
Now your project has something you can visit, share, and tweak. You can open it in a browser and watch it become slightly more yours.
Day 3 — Customize the content
You replace placeholder text with your own.
Suddenly it's not "a generic demo" anymore. It's a reflection of what you care about: your project, your ideas, your experiments.
Day 4 — Share the link
You send it to someone: a friend, a community, or just future you.
Even if it's small, even if it feels unfinished, you have something out in the world instead of only in your head.
Beyond — Keep iterating
You add more sections, pages, and ideas over time.
Everything big you admire probably started as something small and slightly awkward. A single file like this is enough to get you moving.
